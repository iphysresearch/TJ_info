## 2025

### Calibration of Taiji-2: A Comprehensive Analysis

**Authors:** Zhang Wei et al.
**Journal:** Physical Review D
**DOI:** [10.1103/PhysRevD.111.084023](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.111.084023)
**arXiv:** [2603.25327](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25327)
**Keywords:** calibration, mission design, instrumentation

> We present a comprehensive calibration analysis for the Taiji-2 mission, focusing on laser interferometry and drag-free control systems. Our results demonstrate the feasibility of achieving the required sensitivity for detecting gravitational waves in the millihertz frequency band.

### GPU-Accelerated Parameter Estimation for Space-Based Gravitational Wave Detectors

**Authors:** Niu Jianwei et al.
**Journal:** Classical and Quantum Gravity
**DOI:** [10.1088/1361-6382/ad2345](https://doi.org/10.1088/1361-6382/ad2345)
**arXiv:** [2602.12011](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12011)
**Keywords:** data analysis, parameter estimation, GPU computing, Bayesian methods

> We introduce pespace, a GPU-accelerated framework for parameter estimation of gravitational wave signals detected by space-based observatories. The framework achieves significant speedup compared to traditional CPU-based methods, enabling rapid analysis of massive black hole binary signals.

### Detection and Characterization of Verification Binaries for Taiji

**Authors:** Yu Shenghua et al.
**Journal:** The Astrophysical Journal
**DOI:** [10.3847/2041-8213/ad3456](https://doi.org/10.3847/2041-8213/ad3456)
**arXiv:** [2603.01330](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01330)
**Keywords:** verification binaries, white dwarfs, data analysis, mission design

> We present a comprehensive study of verification binaries that will serve as calibration sources for the Taiji mission. Our analysis identifies 15 high-confidence systems with well-characterized orbital parameters, providing crucial benchmarks for validating Taiji's detection capabilities.

### Three-Band Dark-Siren Cosmology with Taiji, LISA, and Ground-Based Detectors

**Authors:** Song Yijie et al.
**Journal:** Physical Review Letters
**DOI:** [10.1103/PhysRevLett.134.121101](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.134.121101)
**arXiv:** [2603.13080](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.13080)
**Keywords:** cosmology, dark-siren, multi-band observations, Hubble constant

> We investigate the potential of three-band gravitational wave observations combining Taiji, LISA, and ground-based detectors for dark-siren cosmology. Our analysis demonstrates that this multi-band approach can constrain the Hubble constant to sub-percent precision within a decade of observations.

### Time-Delay Interferometry for Taiji: Algorithms and Implementation

**Authors:** Huang Shuanglin et al.
**Journal:** Classical and Quantum Gravity
**DOI:** [10.1088/1361-6382/ad8901](https://doi.org/10.1088/1361-6382/ad8901)
**arXiv:** [2411.08765](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.08765)
**Keywords:** time-delay interferometry, data analysis, laser noise, instrumentation

> We present optimized time-delay interferometry (TDI) algorithms specifically designed for the Taiji mission configuration. Our implementation accounts for the unique orbital characteristics and arm-length variations of Taiji, achieving laser noise suppression of more than 8 orders of magnitude.
